Thalassemia

Thalassemia is an inherited blood disorder that causes your body to have less hemoglobin than normal. Hemoglobin enables red blood cells to carry oxygen. Thalassemia can cause anemia.

Thalassemia Type graph


In The Above Graph the numbers Represents:

0 = Safe

1 = fixed defect (no blood flow in some part of the heart)

2 = normal blood flow

3 = reversible defect (a blood flow is observed but it is not normal)

Thalassemia Type graph Based on Target


People with normal blood flow i.e. thal value '2' are more likely to have heart disease.
